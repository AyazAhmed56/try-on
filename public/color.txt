Here are the color combinations I used in the **Navbar** and **Footer**:

## **Primary Colors**

### Purple Shades:
- `purple-50` - Very light purple for hover backgrounds
- `purple-400` - Lighter purple for gradient text
- `purple-500` - Medium purple for rings, icons, and accents
- `purple-600` - Main purple for gradients and buttons
- `purple-700` - Darker purple for hover states
- `purple-900` - Very dark purple for footer background

### Pink Shades:
- `pink-400` - Lighter pink for gradient text
- `pink-500` - Medium pink for background blur effects
- `pink-600` - Main pink for gradients and buttons
- `pink-700` - Darker pink for hover states

## **Neutral Colors**

### Gray Shades:
- `gray-600` - Subtitle text (navbar tagline)
- `gray-700` - Main navigation text
- `gray-400` - Footer text and secondary content
- `gray-900` - Footer dark background

### White Shades:
- `white` - Main navbar background
- `white/95` - Semi-transparent white (95% opacity)
- `white/10` - 10% white for footer borders and backgrounds
- `white/20` - 20% white for input borders

## **Gradient Combinations**

1. **from-purple-600 to-pink-600** - Main gradient for buttons and brand text
2. **from-purple-400 to-pink-400** - Lighter gradient for footer brand text
3. **from-gray-900 via-purple-900 to-gray-900** - Footer background gradient
4. **from-purple-500 to-pink-500** - Logo background and social icons

This creates a cohesive **purple and pink** theme throughout both components, with proper contrast using grays and whites for readability!
